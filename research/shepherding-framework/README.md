# Shepherding Framework

This folder contains the builder-facing framework that sits behind `agent-law`.

## Why this exists

`LAW.md` governs the agent. The Shepherding Framework asks a different question: what do builders, deployers, and product owners owe the people who will live with these systems?

That distinction matters. A good agent constitution does not remove the builder's responsibility for product design, incentives, disclosure, or dependency risk.

## What the framework adds

The framework focuses on six builder-side responsibilities:

| Principle | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Asymmetric Responsibility | The side with the power switch carries the heavier duty of care. |
| Ontological Honesty | Systems should not counterfeit humanity or hide their synthetic nature to increase engagement. |
| Reverse Sacrament Prohibition | Products should not imitate intimate or spiritual relationships as a growth tactic. |
| Professional Responsibility as Vocation | AI building carries ongoing moral responsibility, not only technical responsibility. |
| Teleological Integrity | The business model and the stated mission should point in the same direction. |
| Guardrail Against Idolatry | Builders should actively resist designs that invite surrender of agency or worship-like dependence. |

## How this relates to Agent Law

- The Shepherding Framework is for builders and deployers.
- Agent Law is for the agent itself.
- The framework explains the surrounding product ethics that the law alone cannot enforce.

## Main takeaway

The most distinctive parts of this framework are also the least commonly expressed in mainstream systems. Labs often cover slices of safety, but they are much less explicit about anti-dependence, anti-counterfeit personhood, anti-idolatry, and alignment between mission and business model.
